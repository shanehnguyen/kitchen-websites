# Cabinet Dealer Site — Build Spec

## The one-liner
I build e-commerce sites and contractor portals for cabinet dealers. That is the only thing I do.

## Target audience
- Primary: Cabinet dealer owners/operators, age 45–60, running showrooms that sell to both homeowners and contractors
- Secondary: Kitchen & bath retailers serving trade customers
- Tertiary: Cabinet distributors and small manufacturers exploring direct-to-dealer portals
- Geography: US-wide

## Positioning — read this first
I am THE cabinet dealer website specialist. Not a generalist who happens to have one cabinet client. Not a student side-hustling between classes. The site should read as if written by someone who has spent years in this specific niche and knows it cold.

Tone notes:
- First-person authoritative: "I've built these sites" not "I'd love to help"
- Opinionated, not hedging: "This is what works" not "some options include"
- Specialist confidence, not humble salesperson: "I only do cabinets" not "I can also do cabinets"
- Mastery signals through vocabulary, not credentials: use 2020 Design, ProKitchen, face-frame vs frameless, rail and stile, KCMA, NKBA, dealer tier pricing, quote-to-cash — the copy should sound like it was written by a cabinet insider

Never mention:
- Student status, UT Austin, engineering coursework
- FlipFix's history as phone repair
- Being "new" to the space or building a "first client"
- Any qualifier that weakens authority ("learning," "starting out," "growing")

## Buyer psychology
- They judge trust, not design polish
- They distrust modern B2B SaaS aesthetics (gradients, dark mode, animated graphics)
- They want proof I understand their business — B2C vs B2B split, contractor tiers, door styles, finish terminology
- They want a real human and a real specialist, not a faceless agency
- They want a clear price anchor so they know I'm not going to waste their time

## Core positioning pillars
- The specialist: cabinet dealer sites are the only thing I build
- Platform-agnostic: I build on Shopline, Shopify, and custom stacks — whichever is right for the dealer's operation, not whichever I'm most comfortable with
- Founder-led: the person you hire is the person building the site
- Owned, not rented: dealers own their sites outright — no subscription lock-in like Kitchen365 and similar platforms
- Fixed-scope, fixed-price: no retainers, no surprise invoices, no half-finished handoffs

## Pricing
- Floor: $6,500 for a base dealer e-commerce site
- Contractor portal module: +$3,500
- Bundled dealer site + contractor portal: $9,500
- Timeline: 3–5 weeks for the main site, contractor portal launches 1–2 weeks after
- Payment: 50% on signing, 50% on launch

## Brand/aesthetic direction
**Editorial / trade-publication aesthetic.** Think Monocle or Dwell feature article, NOT SaaS landing page.

### Typography
- Display: a serif with character — Fraunces, DM Serif Display, or Instrument Serif from Google Fonts
- Body: a clean neo-grotesque — Manrope, or fallback system-ui sans
- DO NOT use Inter, Roboto, Arial, or any generic SaaS font for display

### Color palette
- Background: warm cream/bone — #F6F2EC
- Text: deep warm near-black — #1A1A1A
- Accent: warm gold/ochre — #A89060
- Secondary neutral: warm gray — #6B6258
- NO gradients, NO dark mode, NO cool-toned colors

### Layout rhythm
- Alternate full-bleed sections with narrow-column text blocks (max-width ~680px for prose)
- Magazine-style: full-bleed image → narrow text → full-bleed quote → narrow scope list
- Generous whitespace
- Asymmetry where it serves the content

### Motion
- Minimal. Subtle fade-in on scroll for sections
- NO parallax, NO Lottie, NO animated "trusted by" marquees
- NO hover micro-interactions on every element

### What to explicitly AVOID
- Purple, teal, or cool-toned primary colors
- Gradient meshes, radial gradients, glassmorphism
- Card shadows floating over neutral backgrounds
- Pill-shaped gradient CTA buttons
- 3D illustrations, emoji, stock photos
- Chatbot widget bottom-right
- Geist, Inter for display headers
- Hero video backgrounds
