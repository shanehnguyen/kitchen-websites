## THE ONE-LINER

> The owner of a kitchen & bath remodeling or showroom business — main revenue from homeowners — whose craft outclasses his marketing, who built everything on word of mouth, whose leads went soft in the last 18 months for the first time in years, and who has already been burned by at least one lead platform or SEO company.

## 1. WHO (Definition + Money Floor)

A US-based kitchen & bath **dealer/showroom or remodeling contractor** whose **primary revenue comes from homeowners**, not B2B/contractor accounts.

**Excluded on purpose:** businesses whose revenue is mostly contractor/B2B accounts (PK Cabinet-type dealers). The offer doesn't serve that half of their business. Do not soften this filter when outbound gets quiet — serving both genericizes the copy and kills the edge.

**Money floor: ~$750k+/yr revenue [MY CALL].** The math under it is verified:
- Median kitchen renovation spend: ~$24,000 (up 20%); top 10% of projects: $180,000+ (NKBA-derived industry data)
- Mid-size projects can run up to ~$99,000 (NKBA / HBS Dealer)
- At these tickets, one closed job pays the FlipFix engagement multiple times over. Below ~$750k/yr, the buyer is surviving job-to-job and can't sustain a retainer.

**Geography: National (USA)** — earned by FlipFix's existing national kitchen/bath client base, not assumed.

## 3. EXPERIENCES (Strongest layer — the Godfather Offer is built here)

### Situational [VERIFIED]
- **Built on referrals with little/no formal marketing.** So standard that kitchen/bath businesses-for-sale listings advertise "built primarily on word-of-mouth and repeat clients, no formal advertising or website presence" as the norm (BizQuest listings).
- **The market just turned on him.** Q3 2025 US Remodeler Index: incoming leads weak for 54% of design-build firms; backlogs shrank from 4.8 to 3.5 months as homeowners hesitated on discretionary remodels (Qualified Remodeler). The post-COVID backlog era spoiled him; the referral-only model is failing him *now* — this is FlipFix's timing tailwind.
- **He has neglected the free basics.** Study of 85,826 US remodeling contractors: ~60% have ≤5 Google reviews, 26% have zero, 22% never verified their Google Business Profile (YDOP). The wedge, quantified.

### Product-based — THE BURNS [VERIFIED]
1. **Angi / HomeAdvisor.** Federally documented: the FTC found businesses lost time and money pursuing leads who weren't ready to hire, didn't want the service, or weren't in the area — and ordered up to $7.2M paid for deceptive lead-selling practices (FTC, 2023).
2. **Houzz Pro — the kitchen/bath-specific burn.** Widespread review pattern (Capterra, Sitejabber, ConsumerAffairs): subscriptions costing more than they returned; 12-month non-cancellable contracts; unqualified leads. One kitchen reno firm reported ~85% of leads never responding and mostly low-budget vanity inquiries — *anecdote, not a market stat; don't quote it as one.*
3. **SEO companies.** Open contempt on trade forums (ContractorTalk): pitches with no guaranteed results and advice so generic owners had already done it themselves; constant scam cold-calls have poisoned the entire category.

**Implication:** the buyer's most common objection ("marketers ripped me off") is not a hunch — it is federally documented and review-site-verified. The Godfather Offer must be the **anti-Houzz**: no long lock-in, no lead-quantity promises, fast visible result, risk on FlipFix.
