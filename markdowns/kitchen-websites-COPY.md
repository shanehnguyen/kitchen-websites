# Kitchen Websites — Full Site Copy (v1)
### Written in Sabri Suby / King Kong direct-response voice, run through stop-slop, bulletproofed against the buyer's scam radar and the FTC. June 2026.

**How to use this doc.** Every page below is mapped to your screenshots (Image 1 = Home, 2 = Rescue, 3 = Rebuild, 4 = Run, 5 = About). Each section is labeled with the on-screen element so you can drop copy in 1:1. Headlines and CTAs give 2–3 options; everything else is final copy. Margin notes in *italics* name the persuasion move so you can see the logic, not so you ship the note.

**The two rules I would not break, and why:**
1. **No invented proof.** King Kong runs "$7.8 billion generated" and "we guarantee results." You have no client numbers yet and a results/ranking guarantee is the one signal that ends you in a referral niche (and trips the FTC). Every number in here is real and sourced in the ledger at the bottom. Anywhere a testimonial or result belongs, you'll see `{{ PLACEHOLDER }}`. Do not ship those filled with anything but the truth.
2. **Risk reversal lives on what you control.** Deadlines, deliverables, terms, money. Never on rankings or lead counts. That's what lets you make King-Kong-sized promises you can actually keep.

---

## THE COPY FRAMEWORK (the spine every page follows)

Sabri's long-form sequence, adapted to a service the buyer has been burned buying:

1. **Magnetic headline** — enter the conversation already in his head (he's losing jobs and doesn't know why).
2. **Agitate** — make the loss vivid and specific. Put him in the room where it happens.
3. **Name the villain** — it isn't his work, it's the people who robbed him (Angi, Houzz, SEO cold-callers). Side with him.
4. **The mechanism** — The Referral Capture System. The named, unique path out.
5. **Proof / the receipts** — verified numbers and real screenshots. A number or a name, every time.
6. **The Godfather Offer** — named, itemized stack with stated dollar values, then a price that feels like a rounding error against one job.
7. **Risk reversal** — the guarantee, printed big, in plain English.
8. **Qualify** — who it's for, who it isn't. Disqualifying raises desire.
9. **Scarcity** — real capacity caps, never fake countdowns.
10. **Handle objections** — the FAQ does the closing.
11. **Cost of inaction + restate offer + one CTA.**

Cold traffic never hits an offer page first. Every cold ad points at the **Score** ("See how you stack up — 3 minutes"). The offers are for people already warm. Keep that congruence.

### Placeholders you must fill with the truth (never fabricate)
- `{{ FOUNDER_PHOTO }}` — real photo of the real person, or omit.
- `{{ X of 4 / 2 / 10 slots }}` — the true open-slot count, updated as it changes.
- **Testimonials / client results** — none are written into this copy on purpose, because you don't have them yet. The first Rescue clients are your case-study factory. The moment you have a real before/after with a real name and a real number, it goes on the homepage above "The Work," and it will outperform anything in this doc. Until then, the verified market stats carry the proof load.

### Compliance guardrails baked into this copy
- **No ranking guarantee anywhere.** The Run guarantee is on a tracked activity number you control and can verify, not on Google rankings.
- **No lead-quantity promise.** The whole letter rejects "magic leads" by name. That rejection is the trust move.
- **No invented numbers, names, or quotes.** FTC truth-in-advertising applies to you too, and in a referral niche one inflated claim is fatal.
- **Risk reversal only on what you control:** deadlines, deliverables, terms, money.

### Stop-slop pass (applied)
No em-dashes. Tricolon "verified, rebuilt, photo-loaded" loops broken up. The "X. Not Y." reversal used twice on the whole site, both earned by story. Rhythm varied. Every sentence has a human subject doing something. Scenes over declarations.
