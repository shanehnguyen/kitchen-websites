# Kitchen Websites — Site Overview (context for ads / HVCO)

Use this as the "what does my traffic land on" brief when writing ads, HVCOs, emails, or landing copy. Match the voice, point to the right page, promise only what the site backs up.

## What it is
**Kitchen Websites** (kitchenwebsites.com, by FlipFix Digital) — Websites & Google for **kitchen & bath businesses only**. Founder/operator: **Shane Nguyen**, who does the work himself.

## Who it's for
Owners of kitchen & bath / remodeling shops who:
- do **good work** and serve **homeowners** (not mostly builder/contractor/wholesale accounts), and
- are **losing jobs online** to shops that do worse work.

The core insight: their referrals and Google traffic already exist. Homeowners get their name, then check them on Google and on their website *before they call*. Bad search presence or a generic brochure site loses jobs they already earned. **The problem isn't leads. It's a leak.**

## What it sells (two offers)
1. **SEO / Google** (`/seo`) — get found and get picked when a homeowner checks you. Guarantee framing: *more homeowners calling you in 90 days, or you stop paying.* No promise of a #1 ranking or a lead count.
2. **Converting Website** (`/websites`) — a "see-it-first" build. Shane designs the homepage first; *you see it before you pay a dollar* and walk free if you don't love it. You own everything.

## The one destination: the free 30-minute strategy session
**Every CTA on the site points to `/book`.** That is the conversion goal for all traffic.
- `/book` is a King Kong–style multi-step funnel (intro → name → email → kitchen-&-bath qualifiers → details) that ends on a **Calendly** calendar.
- The promise of the session: Shane shows where their jobs are leaking online and hands them **a plan in writing, theirs to keep even if they never hire him.** No pitch-slap.
- Microline used everywhere: *"30 minutes. Kitchen and bath only."*

**So: ads/HVCOs should drive to `/book`** (or to an offer page that then drives to `/book`). The HVCO is effectively the strategy session itself — a free teardown + written plan.

## Voice & non-negotiables (keep ads consistent with these)
- First-person **"I" / Shane** voice, signed *— Shane Nguyen, Kitchen Websites*.
- Honesty brand, anti-lead-gen. The persuasion device is **"they did X, I do Y"** contrast, never "no X, no Y" listing.
- **Risk sits on Shane** (deadlines he pays for missing, a number he doesn't get paid for unless he moves it, see-it-first builds). Only guarantee what Shane controls.
- **Never invent stats, testimonials, reviews, or results.** It's a referral/trust niche (FTC truth-in-advertising). Real proof only; mark placeholders, never fabricate.
- **No Google-ranking guarantees and no lead-quantity guarantees.**
- **No em dashes in body copy** (use commas/periods). A signature em dash is fine.
- Buyer-outcome headlines + a best-case/worst-case close ("worst case, a free plan; best case, you stop losing jobs you already earned").

## Quick rules of thumb for an ad/HVCO
- **Audience:** kitchen & bath shop owners serving homeowners. Disqualify wholesale-only.
- **Angle:** stop losing the jobs you already earned (the leak), not "get more leads."
- **Offer/CTA:** free 30-minute strategy session, plan in writing yours to keep → `/book`.
- **Proof:** real only. If you don't have it yet, don't claim it.
