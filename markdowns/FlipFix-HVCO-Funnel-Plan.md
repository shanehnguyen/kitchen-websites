## THE DECISION (locked this session)

**One front door, comparison-framed:** a 3-minute, 10-question self-benchmark — public-facing hook *"How does your Google presence stack up against your competitors?"* — internally **The Referral Capture Score™**, feeding a dynamic results page, segmented email follow-up, and a warm escalation to the personal head-to-head teardown.

All other HVCO assets deploy at later funnel stages. One opener. No parallel front doors at launch.

---

## WHY (the research verdict, compressed)

1. **The classic Suby HVCO (gated PDF) is structurally dying.** Gated eBooks ~0.9–3.8% conversion vs. interactive tools 5.2%+; calculators lead B2B at ~8.3%; interactive converts ~2.4–6x static; most downloaded PDFs are never opened. (HubSpot 2024/25 data; Demand Gen Report; format benchmark compilations.)
2. **AI finished the job.** 2025–26 consensus: gate only what AI can't replicate — personalized diagnostics, calculators, proprietary data. Information is free; **diagnosis is the HVCO.** Partial ungating beats full gating (+78% in controlled testing).
3. **The apex precedent is a diagnostic:** HubSpot's Website Grader — 4M+ sites graded 2006–2011, foundational to a billion-dollar growth engine, powered by competitive comparison.
4. **The modern system is Scorecard Marketing (Priestley):** short assessment → dynamic personalized results → segmented follow-up. The upgrade over Suby: the PDF captures an email; **the scorecard captures the diagnosis** — every answer is sales intelligence that personalizes the rest of the funnel.
5. **The niche ground is empty.** Adjacent contractor agencies all run the commoditized "free marketing audit" ($2,000-value!! framing — a sales call in costume). KABMS runs webinars/guides/CEU courses (static education). **Nobody in kitchen & bath runs a grader or scorecard.** FlipFix builds web tools for a living — the one HVCO format competitors structurally can't ship.
6. **The comparison hook is triple-validated:** the dossier's defensive-status wound (losing jobs to the worse craftsman with 80 reviews), Priestley's peer-comparison mechanic, and HubSpot's against-your-competition framing. Rivalry beats self-improvement for a man proud of his work.

## FINAL BULLETPROOF — "WOULD THEY ACTUALLY USE IT?"

| Risk | Verdict | Patch |
|---|---|---|
| "Quiz" reads as a toy to a 55-year-old owner | Real — quiz data skews consumer/SaaS | He never sees the word "quiz." Language: **"Check how you stack up"** — blue-collar verbs, zero BuzzFeed energy. Core insurance: the tool digitizes a behavior he ALREADY has (checking out competitors obsessively) — not asking for a new one |
| Self-assessment credibility gap ("it told me what I typed in") | Real | Results benchmark against EXTERNAL verified data (26% zero reviews; top-3 map-pack reality) — him vs. the market, not him vs. himself. Skeptics get the escalation: "Don't take the quiz's word for it — name your competitor, I'll run the real side-by-side." Skepticism becomes the bridge |
| Meta drop-off (leisure mode, leaving FB, load time) | Real | Mobile-first instant-load page, progress bar, 3-minute promise honored literally, email gate AFTER questions / before results (sunk cost earns the ask) |
| Scorecard vanity metrics (high opt-ins, soft leads) | Real | Qualification baked into questions (review count, lead flow, money signals). Funnel judged on **cost per booked call**, never opt-in rate |
| CPL economics unknown | Unpatchable by design | Only ~30 days of spend answers it. Launch is the final stress test |
| Funnel dies at follow-up, not at the HVCO | The actual weakest link | Operations risk with Jack's name on it: same-day lead response + sequences live BEFORE spend starts |

**Verdict: GO** — provided language stays blue-collar, rivalry leads, experience is fast.

## ASSET SPECS & DEPLOYMENT STAGES

### 1. The Referral Capture Score™ — FRONT DOOR (build: weekend)
- Public hook: competitor comparison. Internal architecture: 10 questions, each mapping to a dossier wound AND a Rescue deliverable.
- Question set (locked draft): reviews count · competitor's reviews · GBP login control · last job photos posted · inquiry response speed · revenue source mix · burned-by-marketing history · website real-work vs stock · map-pack presence · lead flow vs 2 years ago.
- Results: score /100, external benchmarks, 3 named leak points, personalized Referral Leak Report (the PDF reborn as dynamic output), dual CTA.
- Data captured = segmentation engine for everything downstream.

### 2. The Lost Referral Calculator — AD CREATIVE + NURTURE (build: an afternoon)
- 3 inputs (referrals/month · avg job value · review count) → annual leakage estimate.
- Math shown openly; assumptions stated conservative (buyer distrusts black boxes).
- Dual role: strongest Meta creative hook + email 2–3 of nurture ("your own numbers, not my claim").
- Failure patch: small-leak results always pair with the Score CTA.

### 3. The K&B Google Grader — WARM ESCALATION (build: template only; automate later)
- Wizard-of-Oz v1: name + email in → templated personal teardown out within 24h (≤20 min using Rescue checklist).
- Publicly capped (true scarcity). Never offered cold — the 24h delay is a feature warm ("he does this personally") and a bounce-risk cold.
- Full automation deferred until volume demands it. Do not gate launch on the software build.

### 4. "Is Your Marketing Guy Ripping You Off?" — CAMPAIGN TWO (post-validation)
- 7-question diagnostic for the burned segment; results deploy FTC HomeAdvisor record + Houzz patterns as receipts; segues to Handshake Terms.
- Own front door, own sequence, shared back end. Launches only after the Score funnel is proven — one valence/intensity zone per campaign.

### 5. The State of Kitchen & Bath Google Presence — THE MOAT (quarter two)
- Original study: sample ~200 K&B profiles, publish findings.
- Partially ungated (findings public + AI-citable; raw data gated) per hybrid-gating evidence.
- PR pitch to KBDN · group-share fodder · proprietary stats feeding every sequence · the authority answer to KABMS's badge wall.

### KILL LIST (validated)
- "Free marketing audit" as a headline — commoditized; the avatar smells the sales call. (The audit lives on renamed and demonstrated: X-Ray / Grader.)
- Generic gated ebook/guide front door — AI-replicable, unread, sub-4% and falling.
- Webinar-first — KABMS's owned ground; wrong attention economics for this buyer.
- "Free consultation" as bait — bottom-of-funnel CTA in costume.
- Email courses / template vaults / CEU plays — not wrong, just LATER or LOWER (premiums, nurture content, future authority).
