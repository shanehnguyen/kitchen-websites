# FlipFix Digital — The Godfather Offer System (v2, Stress-Proofed)
### SLC Phase 6 deliverable · Built on the Dream Buyer Dossier · June 2026

**Built from:** FlipFix services (website builds $3.5–6k+, GBP management, local SEO, monthly retainer) mapped against the dossier's verified wounds (Houzz/Angi burns, SEO-scam distrust, lead softness, neglected Google basics).

**Governing rules (from the stress test):**
1. Reverse risk ONLY on what FlipFix controls — deliverables, deadlines, terms, money. NEVER rankings (Google itself flags ranking guarantees as a scam signal).
2. Specific beats vague. KABMS hides its guarantee in legal terms; FlipFix prints its fine print in headline type.
3. Sell the destination, not the plane (Suby). Every promise leads with the outcome.
4. The Godfather Offer is for the ~3% ready to buy now. The free audit (HVCO) leads everything cold. Never pitch these offers cold.

## THE NAMED MECHANISM

> ## The Referral Capture System™
> *"Word of mouth brings them to Google. We make sure Google brings them to you."*

**Why this name [MY CALL — alternates below]:** The dossier's core insight is that his referrals aren't disappearing — they're dying on his Google page (91% check reviews; Google is the verification layer; 60% decide within 72 hours). "Capture" tells him the system protects what he already earns — it doesn't promise magic leads, the exact promise that burned him.

**The system's three stages (each stage = one offer):**
1. **RESCUE** — fix the Google presence where referrals die (GBP)
2. **REBUILD** — the website that closes the ones who dig deeper
3. **RUN** — keep the number climbing, month over month

Alternates if Capture doesn't sit right: *The Verified Referral Method* / *The Found-&-Chosen System*. Pick one, then never waver — the name compounds only with repetition.

## OFFER 0 — THE WRAPPER: "Handshake Terms"
*Terms layer on every offer. The anti-Houzz. Stated everywhere, loudly.*

- **Month-to-month everything. Cancel any month. No cancellation fee.**
- **You own it all** — site, domain, Google profile, content, photos. You leave, it leaves with you.
- **Plain-English reporting** — what was done, what it cost, what it produced. No vanity dashboards.
- **The fine print is public.** Every guarantee's conditions sheet is shown BEFORE signing, in plain language.

**The copy line:** "No 12-month contract. No hostage website. You own everything I build, and you can fire me any month. I have to earn the next month, every month."

### Stress patches applied
| Failure mode | Patch |
|---|---|
| "No contract" sounds like the $50/mo template mills | NEVER state terms without proof beside them. The weld: "No contract — because my case studies do the retaining." Premium signals (niche case study, named system, real portfolio) must surround the terms |

Here's what months 1–6 actually look like, so you know what you'd be walking away from." + visible weekly work + monthly result emails |
| Churn risk to FlipFix cash flow | Accepted cost of trust. Mitigated by results cadence, the bounded guarantees below, and capacity caps that keep revenue per client high |

## OFFER 2 — THE CORE: "The See-It-First Website Build"
*Stage 2. The $3.5–6k website with the risk sequence inverted. Suby's "results in advance" in its purest form.*

### The promise
> "I'll design your new homepage **first** — your work, your market, your business — before you've committed a dollar to the build. **You see it. Then you decide.** Love it: we build the rest at a flat quoted price, on a 21-business-day clock with real penalties on me if I'm late. Don't love it: you walk, and it cost you nothing."

### Price + payment terms [MY CALL]
- Flat quote, $3,500–6,000+ by scope, fixed before build starts (no hourly creep — he's been burned by open-ended billing).
- Milestones: **40% on go / 40% at content-complete / 20% at launch.**

### Structure & sequence
1. **Qualifying call** (mandatory — money floor, avatar fit, design direction set). This call is where he tests whether you get his business; the spec homepage is your answer.
2. **Spec homepage delivered** as a watermarked image (never files). One revision round on the spec.
3. **Decision.** Walk = walk, genuinely. Go = flat quote + milestones.
4. **Build clock: 21 business days from Content-Complete Day** (the day all his content/photos/approvals are in).
5. **Late penalty: 10% off the project price per full week late, capped at 50%.**

### The value stack (named premiums)
| Premium | What it is |
|---|---|
| **The Punch List Period** | 90 days of post-launch fixes and tweaks, free. (His language — every job ends with a punch list; yours does too.) |
| **The Job Photo Playbook** | Shot list + phone guide so his crew captures portfolio-grade job photos forever |
| **Launch-Day Google Sync** | Site and GBP aligned at launch (services, NAP, links, schema) |

### Conditions sheet
1. The build clock starts at **Content-Complete Day**, not signing day. (The #1 cause of late websites is missing client content — the clock can't start until your materials are in. You'd run your jobs the same way.)
2. The clock pauses on approvals outstanding more than 48 hours.
3. The spec stage includes one revision round; design direction is set on the qualifying call.
4. You own everything at every stage you've paid for.

### Scarcity + reason-why (true)
- "**Two spec-design slots a month.** I design them personally."
- "I design first because you've paid up front for marketing before and gotten nothing. I'd rather carry the risk than ask you to."

### Stress test
| Failure mode | Severity | Patch | Residual risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Spec bleed — design hours on prospects who walk | High | Hard qualification (money floor + fit + direction call); 2/month cap; prefer warm (post-Rescue) over cold | Bounded: max ~2 unconverted specs/month worst case |
| Mockup theft (takes the design to a cheap builder) | Medium | Watermarked flat image only, never files. Residual leakage accepted as advertising — the stolen mockup proved your skill to a man who'll remember when the cheap build disappoints | Accepted |
| Late penalty triggered by client-caused delays | FATAL if unpatched | Content-Complete clock + 48-hour pause rule | Near zero |

## OFFER 3 — THE RETAINER: "Tracked Calls — or I Work Free"
*Stage 3. The recurring prize. NEVER sold cold — only after a Stage 1 or 2 win.*

### The promise
> "Ongoing management of your Google presence — profile, reviews, local SEO, site. We track **one number together: calls, direction requests, and form fills from your Google presence.** Tracked properly, reported in plain English. **If that number isn't up by day 90, my management fee goes to zero until it is.** I don't get paid for activity. I get paid for a number you can count."

### Price [MY CALL]
**$997/month launch price** (range $750–1,500 by market size; raise on proof). Anchored the same way: less per month than the margin on one captured job per quarter.

### The guarantee mechanics (the plumbing IS the offer)
1. **Instrumentation first.** Onboarding week 1 installs the measurement stack: NAP-safe call tracking, UTM-tagged forms, GBP insights snapshot. No promise without plumbing.
2. **Baseline:** trailing-90-day baseline, year-over-year adjusted where history exists (kitchen demand is seasonal — a Q4 baseline flatters, a spring one buries; the written baseline number goes in the agreement).
3. **No-history profiles** (the 26%-zero-reviews crowd): a 30-day instrumented baseline period runs first, then the 90-day clock starts.
4. **The trigger:** tracked number not above baseline by day 90 → management fee = $0 until it is, **capped at 3 free months.** At the cap, either party walks; he keeps everything (Handshake Terms).

### Conditions sheet (the "warranty card" — stated proudly)
> "Like any warranty, this one has maintenance requirements:"
1. Leads called back **same business day** (your own buyers decide within 72 hours — marketing can't survive an unanswered phone).
2. Review asks made on completed jobs — or FlipFix is authorized to run the asks directly.
3. Access maintained (GBP, site, tracking).
4. No second SEO vendor making conflicting changes mid-engagement.

A contractor respects "warranty void if not maintained" — it's how he sells his own work.

### Scarcity (true)
"**Ten retainer seats.** When they're full, there's a waitlist, because I do the work."

### Stress test
| Failure mode | Severity | Patch | Residual risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline gaming / seasonality disputes | High | Written trailing-90-day baseline, YoY-adjusted, signed at start | Low |
| No baseline exists (neglected profiles) | Medium | 30-day instrumentation period precedes the clock | Low |
| Unbounded free-work liability | FATAL if unpatched | 3-month cap, then clean exit with full ownership | Bounded: worst case = 3 months' labor |
| Attribution war ("that wasn't a real call") | High | The tracked number is mechanical (call tracking + UTMs + GBP insights), not anyone's opinion; defined in the agreement | Low |
| Client doesn't answer his phone | High | Warranty conditions, stated up front with the 72-hour stat as the reason-why | Medium — enforce kindly but enforce |
| Call-tracking numbers wreck NAP consistency | Medium | NAP-safe setup (primary number preserved on GBP; tracking via site/UTM layer) — part of the productized onboarding | Low |
| Sounds like every burned promise when pitched cold | FATAL | NEVER pitched cold. Sequence is the persuasion: Rescue win → this offer | Zero if sequence held |

## THE OFFER FLIPFIX DOES NOT RUN: Pay-Per-Lead
It reads like the ultimate risk reversal. It is Angi's costume — the FTC-documented model that burned him (businesses paying per lead for people who weren't ready to hire). It also creates attribution wars no solo operator can referee, and chains FlipFix's income to HIS answer rate. **When a prospect asks for it — and one will — the refusal is a trust move:** "That's the model that burned you. I won't run it. Here's what I do instead, and why my number can't be faked."

| Risk | Why it's real | Mitigation |

| **Guarantee collision** — multiple deadline clocks running at once, solo | 4 Rescues + 2 builds + 10 retainers is a real month for one person | The capacity caps are LOAD-BEARING, not marketing. Never oversell a slot. Hire against the caps (King's Audit: designers take delivery first) |

| **KABMS outguns on proof** (50+ clients, 75X ROI case studies, NKBA badges, team page) | Verified — they run the same playbook with more ammunition | Never compete on breadth or scale claims. Compete where they're vague: specific public guarantees, Handshake Terms, insider build-proof (PK case study), demonstrated video audits. ALSO: join NKBA — the badge is purchasable credibility your competitor weaponizes |
| **No FlipFix case-study numbers yet** | KABMS leads with 34-leads-in-60-days stories; FlipFix has method + portfolio, not metrics | Lead with method, before/afters, and the burns research. NO invented numbers, ever — FTC truth-in-advertising applies to FlipFix too, and one inflated claim in a referral niche is fatal. First Rescue clients are case-study factories: instrument everything |
| **Cash-flow whiplash** (month-to-month + flat fees) | No lock-ins means revenue must be re-earned monthly | The ladder is the mitigation: Rescue cash up front, build milestones mid, retainer seats compound. Monthly result emails do the retaining |
| **Scope creep on flat fees** | "While you're in there, can you also..." — every contractor knows this one, which is the disarm | The deliverables checklist IS the scope. Extras quoted separately — "same way you'd write a change order" |
